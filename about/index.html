About

Education

I graduated from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. Club involvement included Aerospace Club (division: UAV IARC Team ) and eSAB (engineering student advisory board). The goal of the IARC team was to build an autonomous UAV to compete in the International Aerial Robotics Competition. The goal of eSAB was to facilitate a relationship between engineering students, engineering RSOs(recognized student organization), and the college of engineering as a whole(faculty and staff).

Research Interest

My research history shows that I’m interested in UAVs, this first began when I was in networking class at the career center in high school. With UAVs I’ve performed HRI studies and have helped advance the UAV IARC team. I believe that HRI research is important because autonomous systems are supposed to aid humans in many different challenges and domains. For the autonomous systems to do their designated actions, humans should be comfortable with them so that they can trust the autonomous system to perform correctly. HRI studies help autonomous system developers ensure users are comfortable with the system potentially minimizing safety risks. Although I’ve focused on HRI research, I would like to shift my focus to the bioinformatics domain. I’ve been involved with mBiTe which focuses on molecular and biochemical telecommunications. Here I began learning about some bioinformatics methods through the projects that I worked on. The goal of WetSpace is to model communication in a wet communication system. I would like to begin working on and performing research on biological problems by applying computer methods.

Work Experience

Recent Work

During my time at UNL, I worked as a undergraduate researcher at NIMBUS and mBiTe. At NIMBUS I worked under Dr. Brittany Duncan, where my main focus was HRI studies using autonomous UAVs. At NIMBUS I was able to expand my knowledge immensely. I learned about ROS, SOLIDWORKS, AmazonMTURK, and ADOBE creative cloud. At mBiTe I worked under Dr. Massimiliano Pierobon. With Dr. Pierobon, I worked specifically on a project called WetSpace, a cybernetic platform for wet information systems space analysis. I also helped maintain and develop WetCoDe and PaRedox. With the mBiTe lab, I learned about full-stack development and wet communication systems.
